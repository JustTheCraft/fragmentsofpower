Size Details

Space

An Object occupies a cube this long in every direction.

Max Height

Objects that have one dimension much longer than the others can extend out of their space slightly, but not have any dimension greater than their max height. (A Human may be taller than 5ft, but no taller than 8ft)

Reach

Creatures this Size will be able to Reach out of their space to make Attacks or Cast Spells on nearby Enemies.

Physical Defense

The Physical Defense of an Object is used to determine how easy it is to hit. For inanimate Objects there are no other modifiers, but Creatures Physical Defense also include their Level, Dexterity, and Armor. (Feats that change Size already account for this in their adjustment to your Physical Defense)

Bulk

Bulk is how hard an Object is to carry based on its Size. Particularly heavy Objects may have a higher Bulk based on their weight. Containers can carry Objects up to their Bulk.

Base HP

The Base HP of an Object represents the HP an inanimate Object has based on its Size alone.

Object HP

An Object's HP is equal to its Base HP × Durability. Objects have a Resistance to all Damage equal to their Durability. Certain materials may also be Vulnerable or resistant to specific types of Damage. (A GM may rule that a wooden bridge is Vulnerable to Fire Damage, but resistant to Cold Damage)

Durability

The Durability of an Object represents the Strength of the Material used.

Falling Objects

Falling Objects deal Damage to both themselves and what they land on based on their Size and the distance they fell. An Object can fall up to 200 ft per Round. Must fall at least 10ft to take damage. Creatures hit by falling objects their Size or larger are knocked Prone.

Weapons

Weapons, including both Natural Weapons and manufactured Weapons, are used to deal Damage in combat. Weapons usually have one Tag, but Expert Training can grant two additional Tags. If you are Untrained in a Weapon you make Attack rolls with it with Disadvantage.

Weapon Size Damage Dice Melee Damage Bonus Ranged Damage Bonus Description
Light d6 1/3 (STR+Level) — Light weapons are held in one hand with little effort
Medium d8 2/3 (STR+Level) 1/3 (STR+Level) Medium weapons are held in one hand
Heavy d10 STR+Level 2/3 (STR+Level) Heavy Weapons require 2 hands to wield properly

Melee

Used to make Melee Attacks within your Reach. Can also be thrown with a 5ft range increment.

Ranged

Used to make Ranged Attacks with a 50ft range increment. Ammunition is drawn as part of the attack unless it has the Loading quality.

Use DEX for melee attack rolls (but not damage) instead of STR.

+1 bonus on your first attack roll of the turn with this weapon.

Increase the weapon’s damage die size by one step.

Deal +5 damage on attacks made as a readied action.

Corrosive

Disarming

+1 bonus on attacks to disarm.

Entangling

Critical hits entangle enemies your size or smaller for 1 round.

Flaming

Damage dice deal Fire damage (STR bonus remains physical).

Frozen

Damage dice deal Cold damage (STR bonus remains physical).

Grasping

Hand and a Half

Doubles the weapon’s range increment.

Must reload every X attacks, but deals +10/X damage.

Nonlethal

Can knock target Unconscious instead of dealing damage when reducing HP below 0.

Paired

Deal +2 damage if you hit with another paired weapon this turn.

Piercing

Reduce the amount needed to score a critical hit by 1.

Returning

Returns to you immediately after being thrown.

Shocking

Silent

Slashing

+1 bonus if you already hit a different adjacent enemy this turn.

Swinging

Deal +2 damage if you missed an attack with it on the previous action.

Throwing

Can be thrown with a 20ft range increment.

Tripping

Armor

Armors have one Tag. Expert Training grants two additional Tags. You can only wear one set of Armor at a time.

Increases the armor’s Durability by 1 (harder to break and more HP).

Thick

Bombs

Basic Bombs

Basic Bombs are Diminutive items treated as Light Weapons with the Throwing tag. Instead of normal damage, they deal elemental damage (Acid, Cold, Electric, or Fire) based on the bomb’s Level to all targets in the area. A separate Ranged Attack roll is required against each target. Bombs are treated like Area Spells for Advantage and targeting.

They do not benefit from damage-increasing Feats or Tags, but do benefit from range or accuracy bonuses. You may create bombs with reduced damage or smaller blast radius. Bombs can also be made with a fuse (up to 10 rounds).

Enchantments

Enchanted Items allow the user to Cast a specific Spell they may not be able to normally. Casting a Spell this way still requires the caster to spend an Action to Cast or Control the Spell as well as the Energy required. When using an enchanted item the Spell may benefit from any Feats you have that would normally be able to Effect it.

Enchanted Items have a Level equal to that of the Spell they replicate. Enchanted Items can also be made to benefit from a Feat the wielder does not have. This increases the Level of the enchanted item by 1.
